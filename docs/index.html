Exploring the crimes in Chicago from 2001 to 2019
Chicago is the third most populated city in the United States with almost 3 millions resident in 2019. The city is located in the State of Illinois, and is classed as a "global city" the Globalization and World Cities Research Network. The Chicago Police Departement started to record the crimes since the year 2001. An astonishing observation is that the dataset containing all the crimes from 2001 to 2019 has more than 7 millions entries!
In this project we present some insight on those data and try to explain them with some socio-economics factors and also the locations of police stations.
Here is the first insight: the mean number of the total number of crimes of all types for each days of the year.
We see that the average number of crimes are generally constant over the observed years. But there are some exceptions!
The most obvious one is the big peaks of crimes the first of January. The parties the night before for the new year's eve must have something to do with it. Something surely has caught your attention as well: there are peaks of crimes for every first of the month! We have no clear explanation about them, only some suppositions. The first one is the naive one: the first of each month has an impact on crime, maybe due to people having received their salary recently or thinking the beginning of a new month is the occasion to do crazy stuff. But surely, even if the dataset is provided by a big city, we believe that those peaks comes from errors in the entries and that if there is no precise date for a crime, the value is defaulted to the first of the month.
Finally, we can see that the day with the lowest crimes rate is christmas, which could be explaind by the fact that the majority of the population of the United States is christian.
